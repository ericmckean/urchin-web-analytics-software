Referring Sources

Which referral sources result in the most revenue? This report shows which referral sources (such as search engines, newsletters, and referral sites) are associated with the most revenue.

Click the button at the far left of any entry to view data over time.

To compare two date ranges, select the "Compare to Past" option on the Calendar tab.

Each source is qualified by medium, in brackets, such as [referral]. Each medium is either one of the mediums with which you tagged referral links or one of the following special cases:

  • [organic] indicates visitors referred by an unpaid search engine listing.
  • [referral] indicates visitors referred by links which were not tagged with any campaign variables.
  • [(not set)]indicates visitors referred by links which were tagged with campaign variables but for which the medium variable was not set.
  • (direct)[(none)] indicates visitors who visited the site by typing the URL directly into their browser.

Revenue is the total revenue resulting from e-commerce transactions by visitors referred by the source. Trans is the number of transactions. $/Trans is Revenue divided by Transactions. Percent $ is the percentage of your site's total e-commerce revenue that visitors referred by the source contribute.
